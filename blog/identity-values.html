Identity and Values

Explain a situation where you have had an ethical decision to make. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and the consequences of your action.

At one of the kitchens I worked at there was a kitchen hand who didn't particularly like the head chef. The head chef treated him terribly because he was slow and always wanted to leave work early so that he could head to his next job as he worked at two different places. This person, lets name him John for privacy sake, asked me for advice about whether he should quit this job or the other job as he really wanted to become a chef. John didn't have experience and he wanted to be a chef badly. Head chef wanted to get rid of him but I asked him to give him a chance. 3 months went by and he was getting better but still receiving a lot of negative feedback from the head chef. By then, John wanted to quit. My advice to John "You're doing fine give it time, everyone learns and progress at different speed and you're also working a full time job elsewhere while working here part time give yourself some credit". He decided to stay and actually improved dramatically, finishing earlier than usual and picked up the pace. However he left this job as he was promoted in his other full time job. In my opinion he was a great kitchen hand and we were short in staff but I wanted what was best for him not for myself or the head chef. The consequence that we had to struggle with a slow kitchen hand for a couple of months but in the end John got he wanted and we all ended in good terms plus a new experienced kitchen hand replacement.
